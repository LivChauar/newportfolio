Translating complex clinical medication data into a clear, actionable risk score that helps pharmacists intervene before adverse events occur.

The Problem

Clinical pharmacists at large health systems were spending an average of 4.2 hours per shift manually reviewing patient medication lists to identify high-risk individuals. The process was inconsistent, time-intensive, and entirely dependent on individual pharmacist expertise — leading to missed interventions and patient safety events.

The product team had access to rich claims and EHR data that could power a predictive model. The challenge: how do you surface a complex algorithmic output in a way that a busy clinician trusts and acts on?

"How might we give pharmacists the right signal, at the right moment, with enough context to act confidently?"

Discovery & Research

I conducted 14 contextual inquiry sessions across 3 health systems, shadowing pharmacists during their shifts. Key findings:

  • Pharmacists trusted their instincts over algorithms they couldn't interrogate.
  • Numeric scores alone created anxiety — they needed to understand the "why."
  • Time pressure meant any new tool had to fit into an existing workflow in under 30 seconds per patient.
  • Color-coding for risk had accessibility implications that existing tools ignored.

Design Decisions

The core design principle was explainability over confidence. The risk score is never shown as a single number. Instead, it surfaces as a tiered indicator (Low / Moderate / High) accompanied by the top 3 contributing factors, each with a plain-language rationale.

I designed a progressive disclosure model: the summary card lives in the existing patient list view, while a detailed breakdown opens in a side panel — never navigating away from the patient context. This kept the average interaction time under 22 seconds.

Accessibility drove color decisions. I used shape and iconography in addition to color for risk indicators, with a contrast ratio exceeding WCAG AA across all states, verified with real assistive technology testing.

Outcome

  • Adopted by 94% of pharmacists within the first 6 weeks of launch.
  • Average risk-review time dropped from 4.2 hrs to 1.8 hrs per shift.
  • Clinician-reported confidence in intervention decisions increased by 37%.
  • Zero critical accessibility issues flagged in post-launch audit.
