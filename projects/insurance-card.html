Insurance Card

Replacing a physical insurance card with a mobile-first, accessible digital experience that works when it needs to — at the roadside, in a clinic, or in a low-signal zone.

Role Lead Product Designer
Timeline 4 months
Platform iOS + Android
Team 1 Designer, 3 Engineers, 1 PM

The Opportunity

Insurance ID cards are mandated in most US states for vehicle registration and insurance verification. Despite widespread smartphone adoption, millions of people still carry physical cards — often expired, illegible, or simply not on them when needed. State laws are increasingly permitting digital versions, but existing implementations were digital replicas of physical cards: hard to read, inaccessible, and unavailable offline.

The business goal was to migrate 70% of policyholders to digital cards within 12 months. The design challenge was to make the digital experience unambiguously better than the physical one.

"At 2 AM on the side of a highway in the rain, your UI has maybe 4 seconds to work. There is no room for confusion."

Research

We surveyed 1,200 policyholders about their physical card habits and ran usability tests across 3 competitor apps with 16 participants. We also consulted with law enforcement on what they actually need to see during a roadside stop.

  • 68% of users had trouble finding their physical card at the point of need.
  • Competitor apps buried the card 3–5 taps deep — too slow in a high-stress moment.
  • Officers needed 4 specific fields: policy number, insurer, effective dates, and vehicle VIN.
  • 23% of test users had accessibility needs that existing apps completely ignored.
  • Offline availability was a non-negotiable — emergencies don't wait for signal.

Design Solution

The card is accessible from the app home screen in one tap — no authentication required for the card view (we worked with legal to validate this). The card is stored locally on device for full offline access.

The visual design uses a large-type layout optimized for readability at arm's length in poor lighting. We ran contrast tests in direct sunlight and under harsh white light. The 4 key fields are displayed at 24px minimum with a secondary grid for additional policy details.

For users managing multiple vehicles, a card-flip interaction reveals additional vehicles without navigating away. The entire flow was designed with VoiceOver and TalkBack in mind from day one — not as an afterthought. Dynamic Type support means it adapts to any user's system font size preference.

A "Share Card" feature lets policyholders send a secure, time-limited PDF to a third party — useful for loan paperwork, car rentals, and other verification scenarios.

Outcome

  • 74% of active app users adopted the digital card within 60 days of launch — exceeding the 12-month goal.
  • App store rating increased from 3.1 to 4.6 stars, with digital card cited in 38% of positive reviews.
  • Accessibility audit score improved from 61% to 98% WCAG AA compliance.
  • Support contacts related to card retrieval dropped 81%.
