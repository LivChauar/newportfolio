Driving Insights

Making telematics data human — a dual-audience dashboard that helps fleet managers optimize safety and empowers drivers to understand and improve their behavior.

Role Senior Product Designer
Timeline 6 months
Platform Web + iOS
Team 1 Designer, 4 Engineers, 1 PM, 1 DS

The Challenge

Telematics platforms are notoriously numbers-heavy. Insurance companies and fleet operators have access to thousands of driving data points per trip — but most platforms present this as raw tables that neither fleet managers nor drivers know how to act on.

We had two distinct users with different needs: fleet managers wanting aggregate trends and exception alerts, and individual drivers wanting personalized feedback without feeling surveilled. One product had to serve both without alienating either.

"Data without narrative is just noise. Our job was to turn driving behavior data into a story drivers would want to improve."

Research

I ran diary studies with 12 drivers over two weeks and conducted stakeholder interviews with 6 fleet managers. Key tension points:

  • Drivers felt monitored rather than supported — existing tools created anxiety, not motivation.
  • Fleet managers spent most of their time in Excel because the dashboard couldn't answer their actual questions.
  • Both groups distrusted scores they couldn't understand or challenge.
  • The best driving programs used coaching language, not grading language.

Design Solution

I designed a dual-mode dashboard with a shared data layer. Fleet managers access a macro view: fleet health score, top exception categories, driver comparison, and trend lines with configurable date ranges. Everything exportable.

The driver-facing mobile experience uses a coaching metaphor. Instead of a single score, drivers see a weekly "trip report" with three highlights — one strength to reinforce, one area to improve, and one comparison to their own personal best. No peer comparison. No shame mechanics.

Data visualization used a combination of small multiples and annotation layers to show context. Speed violations, for example, are shown on a route map with time-of-day context — giving drivers a way to connect behavior to real situations they remember.

Outcome

  • Driver app weekly active usage grew from 12% to 61% within 3 months.
  • Average driver safety score improved 18% across the beta fleet.
  • Fleet manager task completion rate increased from 44% to 89%.
  • Net Promoter Score for the driver app went from +11 to +52 post-redesign.
